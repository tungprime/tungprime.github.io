Tung T. Nguyen

I am postdoctoral associate at Western University. My postdoc advisors are Professor Ján Mináč and Professor Lyle Muller.

I am an interdisciplinary mathematician. My primary research interests are in computational/algebraic number theory, non-commutative algebras, and nonlinear dynamics. I have also worked on several side projects in spectral graph theory Machine Learning, and Galois modules.

I enjoy finding hidden patterns from experimental data. A large part of my research is inspired and guided by big data and simulations (for example, see here for some data that I generated for our work on Fekete polynomials).

I obtained my Ph.D. at the University of Chicago in December 2020. My advisor is Professor Kazuya Kato.

Before coming to Chicago, I was an undergraduate student at Vietnam National University. I wrote my senior thesis under the supervision of Professor Ralph Greenberg.

Teaching and Mentoring

I have been teaching and mentoring mathematics for more than eight years. As an instructor and mentor, I introduce my students to beautiful and practical mathematical ideas, help them develop critical thinking skills, and provide mentorship to support their success. As a lecturer, I always try to engage and motivate my students in their studies. One of my favorite things in teaching mathematics is using real-life examples to introduce or demonstrate a new concept. I strongly believe that anyone, regardless of race, gender, or social status, can master and enjoy mathematics. Here is a summary of my teaching records.
  • I was an instructor for the course "Probability and Statistics" at Vietnam National University, 2014.
  • I was an instructor for both the regular and advanced calculus sequences at UChicago (Math 131-132-133 and Math 151-152-153) from 2015-2020.
  • In the summer of 2019, I designed a curriculum for and taught a summer course "Introduction to Mathematicstics via proofs-based methods'' for incoming UChicago students thorough the Chicago Academic Achievement Program. It is a memorable experience. Click here for a picture of me with some students in this course.
  • I mentored three students in the Directed reading program and two students in the REU program at UChicago.
  • Co-supervised (joint with Ján Mináč and Lyle Muller) four students in the 2021 Fields Undergraduate Summer Research Program. We are completing a paper on spectral graph theory and matrix algebra.
  • Co-supervised Lewis Glabush toward his senior thesis (joint with Prof. Jan Minac and Prof. Lyle Muller). Topic: Special Families of Generalized Paley Graphs and the Riemann Hypothesis for Graphs.
  • Co-supervised the PhD student Priya B. Chain (joint with Ján Mináč and Lyle Muller). Topic: Patterns in multiplex networks of nonlinear oscillators.
